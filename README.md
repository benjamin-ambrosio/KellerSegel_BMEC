# 🧠 Modeling Brain Microvasculature with Keller–Segel Type PDEs

Author: B. Ambrosio, A.F. Garroudji, S. Fitzsimons, H. Zaag, F.M. Elahi 
Status: Submitted article  
Field: Mathematical Biology / PDE Modeling / Computational Neuroscience  

---

## 📌 Overview
This repository accompanies a submitted research article presenting a partial differential equation (PDE) of Keller–Segel (KS) type that reproduces spatial patterns observed during the growth of brain microvasculature.

The work combines mathematical analysis and data-driven modeling to better understand how vascular structures evolve and how impairments may be linked to neurodegenerative diseases.

---

## 🧠 Abstract
This article introduces a Keller–Segel-type partial differential equation capable of reproducing patterns commonly observed during the development of brain microvasculature. We provide mathematical insights into the mechanisms driving the emergence of these patterns.

In addition, a data-driven equation is derived to ensure a consistent temporal evolution of the chemoattractant associated with the observed vascular dynamics. Beyond numerical simulations, this work contributes toward a comprehensive mathematical modeling framework, spanning blood flow in cerebral arterial networks to biochemical processes. The broader objective is to better understand how vascular dysfunctions may contribute to neurodegenerative diseases.
